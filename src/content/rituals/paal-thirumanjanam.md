A modest but distinct Tamil marriage rite, mostly performed at the threshold of the marital home immediately after the ceremony — though the timing varies between Kongu sub-regions. The bride and groom stand at the doorway; senior female kin pour a mixture of milk, water, and turmeric paste over their feet, and the couple steps over the threshold into a fresh new home.

The dual function is genuinely useful. Turmeric is a documented mild antimicrobial; the foot-wash before entering a home is a literal hygiene step in pre-paved village contexts. The witness function is the larger work: elder women of the household are visibly the ones performing the blessing, formalising their acceptance of the new bride and signalling the household's continuity. Detailed ethnographic treatment of this specific rite in Kongu Vellala households is sparse in academic literature, but community-internal descriptions are consistent across sub-regions. Tiered rational-basis because the hygiene and social-witness functions are defensible non-supernatural explanations for the practice.
