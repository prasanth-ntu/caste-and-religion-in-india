The rule is simple and absolute in traditional practice: two Kongu Vellala individuals sharing a kootam cannot marry, even if no traceable common ancestor exists within living memory. Preferred unions follow the maaman–machan (மாமன்–மச்சான்) cross-cousin pattern across distinct kootams — keeping property and ritual within a defined social universe while crossing the clan boundary.

Stripped of the "ancestors decreed it" framing, the system is a coherent population-genetic strategy. Caste endogamy is the constant; clan exogamy is the optimisation. The rule materially expands the effective mating network of a community that, on the Moorjani and Nakatsuka findings, has been strictly endogamous for roughly two millennia and shows founder-event signatures comparable to Ashkenazi Jews. Whether the rule was designed for this effect or selected for it, the effect is real.
