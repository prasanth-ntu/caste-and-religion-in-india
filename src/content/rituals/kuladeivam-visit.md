For the Kadai Kootam line, the destination is Konur Kaliamman temple in the Kongu region; for each of the 145 kootams, a different specific shrine. Visits cluster around tonsuring, marriage, and the deity's annual festival, but families also travel for births and named feasts.

The visible work the visit does is substantial and uncontroversial: it pulls dispersed kin back to a single physical location at predictable intervals, lets elders verify children and new in-laws against the lineage, and creates a stable point of return for diaspora generations. In a pre-bureaucratic society this is not a small thing — it is the closest analogue to a clan registry that the community had. These functions are tiered yellow because they are well-attested but mostly through community-internal sources and ethnography rather than peer-reviewed studies. The supernatural-causation framing is handled separately as a red-tier claim.
