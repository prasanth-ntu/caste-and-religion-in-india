Splitting this from the kuladeivam-visit entry matters. The visit itself does real and defensible social work — kin reunion, lineage registry, life-cycle anchoring — that this site treats at the yellow tier. The separate claim that the deity inflicts measurable misfortune on non-visitors is a distinct supernatural-causation claim, and it is red-tier.

The pattern is familiar from the broader anthropology of religion: families that experience misfortune after missing a visit interpret the misfortune as caused by the missed visit (confirmation bias); families that experience no misfortune after missing a visit rarely report this counterexample (survivorship reporting); families that experience misfortune after visiting blame insufficient vow-fulfilment rather than the deity. The belief is unfalsifiable as usually stated, and unfalsifiable claims do not earn evidentiary weight. The respectful position is to acknowledge that the belief is sincere and culturally important while declining to assign it explanatory power.
