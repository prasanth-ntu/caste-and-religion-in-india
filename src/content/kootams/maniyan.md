# Maniyan Kulam

Maniyan (மணியன்) is one of the kootams of the Kongu Vellala Gounder community, rooted in the Namakkal district on the banks of the Cauvery.

## The story

<Claim tier="yellow" sources={["globalkongufoundation-maniyan", "maniankulam-blogspot"]}>
The ancestral settlement of this kulam is **Mohanur**, a riverbank town on the Cauvery in Namakkal district. Community oral tradition holds that the clan deity — **Karuppasamy**, venerated here under the epithet **Navaladiyan** ("நாவலடியான்" — "he who sits at the foot of the Naval tree") — manifested beneath an ancient Naval Maram (*Syzygium cumini*, Jamun / Indian Blackberry) on the riverbed. Ancestors who witnessed the event adopted the tree as the kula vriksham (குல விருட்சம், clan-tree) and the site as the seat of worship. Sub-branches named **Muthur Maniyan** and **Thurkatchi Maniyan** are noted in community sources; all are considered *pangalis* (brother clans) for whom inter-marriage remains prohibited.
</Claim>

## The totem

The **Naval Maram** (நாவல் மரம், *Syzygium cumini*) is the *kulachinnam* of this kootam. Three taboos are consistently reported in community oral tradition: 🟡

<Claim tier="yellow" sources={["globalkongufoundation-maniyan", "maniankulam-blogspot"]}>
1. Members do not fell or cut the Naval tree.
2. Its wood is not used as firewood.
3. The first fruit of each season is reserved as an offering to Navaladiyan before household consumption.
</Claim>

The prohibition on cutting shares a structural logic found across Kongu tree-totem kootams: the totem's ecological protection is encoded in a ritual rule, and violation is believed to invite clan misfortune. See also: [the totem-clan-deity triad principle](/claims/kongu-totem-clan-deity-triad).

## The temple today

The Mohanur Navaladiyan temple is the operational kuladeivam site for most Maniyan lineages. Community sources report that the Naval tree — or a direct vegetative offshoot — remains the central focus of worship within the temple precinct, making the botanical-ritual link directly visible.
