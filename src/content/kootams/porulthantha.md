# Porulthantha Kootam

Porulthantha (பொருள்தந்த குலம்) is one of the 145 kootams of the Kongu Vellala Gounder community, rooted in the Kongu Nadu region. Its older, ancestral clan name is **Porulaanthai** (பொருளாந்தை).

## The name and the totem

🟡 *Family-attested.* The clan name **Porulaanthai** derives from **Aanthai** (ஆந்தை — the **owl**), the totem bird (*kulachinnam*) of this kootam. The owl totem links Porulthantha to the broader Kongu bird-totem clan system — compare the neighbouring [Aandai Kootam](/kootams/aandai), whose name likewise carries the owl (Strigidae). As with other Kongu totem clans, the totem animal is traditionally protected by clan members rather than hunted. 🟡

## Kuladeivam

🟡 *Family-attested.* The clan deity (kuladeivam) of Porulthantha lineages is **Malaiyamman** (மலையம்மன்), worshipped at the **Thottakurichi Malaiyamman Temple** at **Punjai Thottakurichi** in **Karur district**, Tamil Nadu (near Pugalur, in historical Kongu Nadu). The temple location is confirmed from a Google Maps pin; the Porulthantha ↔ Malaiyamman binding rests on family oral tradition.

## Exogamy

Per standard kootam practice, Porulthantha members do not marry within their own kootam. Porulthantha is recorded as one of the exogamy partners of the **Kadai Kootam** — the maaman-machan (மாமன்–மச்சான்) cross-cousin alliance between Kadai and Porulthantha is reciprocal, with each clan listed among the other's marriageable partners. 🟡
