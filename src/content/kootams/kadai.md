# Kadai Kootam

Kadai (காடை, meaning the **quail** — *Coturnix coturnix*, a small ground-dwelling bird native to the dry Kongu landscape) is one of the 145 kootams of the Kongu Vellala Gounder community.

## The totem

The quail is the *kulachinnam* (குலச்சின்னம்) of this kootam. Members traditionally do not hunt or eat the bird. Community oral tradition recounts an origin episode in which a quail's alarm call alerted an ancestor to danger — a story characteristic of South Indian totemic clan-myths but not historiographically datable. 🟡

## Kuladeivam

For many Kadai lineages in the Kongu region, the clan deity (kuladeivam) is **Konur Kaliamman**, with annual visits during the Aadi and Panguni Uthiram festivals.

## Exogamy

Per standard kootam practice, Kadai members do not marry within the Kadai kootam. Preferred maaman-machan (மாமன்–மச்சான்) cross-cousin alliances are established with several non-Kadai kootams; the specific pairings vary by sub-region and lineage.
