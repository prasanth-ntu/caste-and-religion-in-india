# Senganni Kootam

Senganni (செங்கண்ணி), also rendered **Sengannan Kulam**, is one of the kootams of the Kongu Vellala Gounder community.

## The story

<Claim tier="yellow" sources={["globalkongufoundation-maniyan"]}>
The name **Senganni** (செங்கண்ணி) is derived in community tradition from "red-eyed / fierce-eyed guardians" — invoking the protective, watchful character attributed to this kootam's deity. The kuladeivam is **Pudhu Vengarai Amman** — "the goddess of the new border of Vengai trees" — whose name encodes both the tree-totem and the territorial-guardian role. The Vengai tree (*Pterocarpus marsupium*, East Indian Kino) yields a dark blood-red sap; community oral tradition reads this as a material sign of the goddess's fierce, protective power.
</Claim>

## The totem

The **Vengai Maram** (வேங்கை மரம், *Pterocarpus marsupium*) is the *kulachinnam* of this kootam. The tree is known for the dark crimson gum it produces when the bark is wounded.

<Claim tier="yellow" sources={["globalkongufoundation-maniyan"]}>
Community tradition holds that before a brick-and-mortar structure was built, the idol of Pudhu Vengarai Amman was placed inside the hollow or exposed root cavity of a massive living Vengai tree. Vengai timber remains barred from ordinary commercial use by Senganni lineages, and grove trees on agricultural land are actively protected.
</Claim>

The tree-hollow shrine origin is a structurally common pattern in South Indian folk-deity traditions, lending the account contextual plausibility, though no inscription or archival record has been located to date it. See also: [the totem-clan-deity triad principle](/claims/kongu-totem-clan-deity-triad).

## The temple today

The deity **Pudhu Vengarai Amman** is worshipped in **Vengarai**, Namakkal district. The temple name — "Pudhu" (new) combined with "Vengarai" (Vengai-tree boundary) — preserves the founding story in its toponym.
